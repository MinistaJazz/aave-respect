# Governance

## Stewardship

AAVE Respect was created by Minista Jazz / Much Different World and is stewarded as a public standard with protective governance.

The goal is adoption without surrender. The code and public documentation are MIT licensed so the standard can spread. The authority to certify, verify, or speak for the project remains separate.

## Decision Principles

Maintainer decisions follow these principles:

1. Protect AAVE speakers from correction, caricature, flattening, and extraction.
2. Treat Black women and Black language communities as originators, not data sources.
3. Keep recognition separate from performance.
4. Prefer careful scope over broad claims.
5. Make the public standard useful without publishing every private evaluation or operator detail.
6. Welcome adoption, but do not let corporate convenience weaken the standard.
7. Prevent governance capture by the loudest, richest, or fastest-moving contributors.

## Maintainer Authority

Maintainers may:

- accept, edit, or reject contributions
- request sources, context, or consent details
- remove examples that expose private people or communities
- decline changes that flatten AAVE or encourage mimicry
- protect private benchmark, evaluation, and conformance materials
- reserve certification, compliance, and trademark claims for separate review

## Change Process

Small documentation fixes may be submitted directly as pull requests.

Larger changes should start as an issue first, especially changes involving:

- new linguistic features
- benchmark structure
- regional or non-US Black English varieties
- speech-to-text guidance
- evaluation thresholds
- certification or compliance language

Maintainers may ask for scholarly sources, community context, or a narrower claim before accepting a change.

## Commercial Use

Commercial use is allowed under the MIT License.

Commercial authority is not granted by the MIT License.

Companies may use the public standard, but they may not claim certification, verification, endorsement, partnership, or compliance unless that has been granted separately in writing.

## Future Conformance Program

A future conformance or certification program may verify implementations against a deeper test suite and allow approved use of a compliance mark.

That program would be separate from this open repository:

- the public code stays open
- the public standard stays inspectable
- verification labor and marks may be paid
- certification claims require review
