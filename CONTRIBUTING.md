# Contributing

AAVE Respect is open because respect has to travel.

It is stewarded by Minista Jazz / Much Different World, and the open license is not an invitation to extract from Black language communities without care.

## What We Welcome

- corrections to linguistic explanations
- additional regional or generational variation, with context
- benchmark prompts that expose real failure modes
- implementation notes for agents, speech-to-text systems, editing tools, and safety reviews
- documentation improvements that make the standard easier to adopt without weakening it

## Before You Submit

Ask these questions first:

1. Does this change protect meaning, consent, and dignity?
2. Does it avoid treating AAVE as a costume, content source, or marketing texture?
3. Does it distinguish evidence from opinion?
4. Does it avoid claiming one feature represents every Black speaker?
5. Does it keep recognition separate from performance?

If the answer is no, pause before opening a pull request.

## Consent And Data

Do not submit private, sensitive, or community-held material without explicit permission.

This includes:

- private recordings or transcripts
- DMs, texts, emails, classroom writing, workplace writing, clinical notes, or legal testimony
- screenshots from private groups
- stories, phrases, or examples shared in confidence
- material involving minors or vulnerable people

If an example is based on a real person, either get permission or rewrite it into a synthetic example that preserves the linguistic point without exposing the person.

When submitting examples, label the source clearly:

- `synthetic`
- `public source`
- `personal/lived example with permission`
- `scholarly source`

## Linguistic Claims

AAVE is not a monolith. Contributions should avoid universal claims like "AAVE speakers always..." or "Black people say..."

Good contributions usually name the scope:

- region
- generation
- context
- register
- source type
- uncertainty

If you are not sure, say so. Careful uncertainty is better than confident flattening.

## Companies And Product Teams

The MIT license allows broad use of the code and documentation. It does not grant certification, endorsement, partnership, or compliance status.

You may say:

- "uses AAVE Respect"
- "based on the public AAVE Respect standard"
- "inspired by AAVE Respect"

Do not say:

- "AAVE Respect compliant"
- "AAVE Respect certified"
- "verified by AAVE Respect"
- "approved by Minista Jazz"
- "endorsed by Much Different World"

Those claims require a separate written agreement or a future conformance program.

If this standard helps your product make money, fund the work. The license is open. The stewardship labor is not yours to drain.

## Licensing

By submitting an issue, pull request, benchmark, or documentation contribution, you agree that your contribution may be released under the MIT License used by this repository.

Only submit material you have the right to contribute.

We do not require a CLA today. We do require clean provenance, consent, and respect for the people whose language this work concerns.

## Pull Request Checklist

Before opening a pull request:

- confirm you have the right to contribute the material
- remove private or identifying details
- include sources or context when making linguistic claims
- mark regional or contextual limits
- avoid compliance, certification, or endorsement claims
- keep examples respectful and non-caricatured
- explain what failure mode the change prevents

Maintainers may edit, decline, or close contributions that create extraction risk, flatten AAVE, or invite misuse.
