# Code Of Conduct

## Purpose

AAVE Respect exists to protect linguistic dignity. That includes the language, the people who speak it, and the Black women whose labor, lineage, and cultural knowledge are too often taken without care, credit, or consent.

This project welcomes serious contributors. It does not welcome extraction, spectacle, or dialect shaming.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- treat AAVE as a rule-governed language variety, not broken English
- speak with care about Black language communities
- respect regional, generational, class, gender, and register differences
- accept correction without turning it into a debate about someone's humanity
- cite sources or name uncertainty when making claims
- protect private examples and community-held knowledge
- keep disagreement focused on the work

## Not Allowed

The following behavior is not allowed:

- anti-Blackness, misogynoir, racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, ableism, or class contempt
- correcting, mocking, or pathologizing AAVE as defective language
- demanding unpaid education from Black contributors
- using AAVE performatively to joke, posture, or provoke
- submitting unconsented private speech, writing, audio, screenshots, or transcripts
- identity policing, including claims about who is "really" Black based on language
- harassment, threats, doxxing, sexualized comments, or sustained unwanted contact
- corporate pressure to weaken protections for adoption, speed, or marketing
- false claims of certification, approval, compliance, or endorsement

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject pull requests, block users, or restrict participation when behavior threatens the project or the people it exists to protect.

For sensitive concerns, email `legal@muchdifferentworld.com`. Do not post private or identifying information in a public issue.

Enforcement decisions prioritize protection over debate. Nobody is owed access to this project at the expense of Black people's dignity.
